You should learn freehand knife sharpening

Give yourself the gift of razor-sharp kitchen knives for life
January 23, 2026

I was gifted a 15-count value kitchen knife set for Christmas a few weeks ago because I want to learn how to become a better cook. Shortly after, I got sucked into the knife-sharpening rabbit hole.

Over the span of about 1-2 hours of YouTube videos and 1-2 hours of hands-on practice, I went from not knowing anything about knives to having kitchen knives so sharp that they can cut hair off of my arm and glide through paper with ease.

I am amazed at just how much more fun it is in the kitchen when you have extremely sharp knives. Suddenly there is nothing stopping you from learning those fancy chef cutting techniques that require a sharp blade. It also makes it more fun to cut up fruit and vegetables, so you find that suddenly you're eating healthier too.

This little foray into knife sharpening has given me such a significant boost to my quality of life in the kitchen that I feel obligated to share how you can have sharp knives too. The payoff is so high for something that takes such little time to learn.

Knife Sharpening, Made Simple

Technique

I am not going to attempt to teach or explain techniques here. Rather, I am only going to show you the resources I utilized to learn. With these resources you will be able to develop the ability to sharpen knives to the point where you can at least shave the hair off of your arm for minimal effort.

The best resource for me to learn, by far, were the videos from OUTDOORS55 on YouTube. His channel is a goldmine of practical knowledge. The great thing about his videos is they all have microscopic shots of the knife edge, so you get to see how the knife edge is changed at every step of the process after each technique that he demonstrates.

Some of the most important videos are:

  1. Sharpening a Kitchen Knife In Real Time - How To Sharpen A Chefs Knife
  2. How To Sharpen A Knife In About 5 Minutes With ONE Stone
  3. Why Your Knife Dulls So FAST! Proper Burr Identification And Removal
  4. 4 Knife Sharpening Tips YOU MUST KNOW For Hair Whittling Edges

There is going to be a lot of repeated information across these videos, but I find that he explains it a bit differently in each video. The repitition also helps the information stick.

Practice

That's it! Just watch those videos, order those tools, and practice. It's important to continually review what you're doing with the Jewelers loupe so that you have visual feedback and can see how your movements change the knife edge. After you practice for a few hours, you should be able to get your knives to a similar sharpness to the levels you see demonstrated in his videos.
